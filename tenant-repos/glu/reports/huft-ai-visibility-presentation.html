Strategic Recommendation

HUFT must pivot from technical SEO to third-party presence to win in AI Overviews

Priority 1: Third-Party Tables Critical

  • Get included in comparison tables on supertails.com, zigly.com, petsworld.in
  • Ensure HUFT appears in "Top 10" and "Best X" lists
  • Target sites Google actually cites (see Exhibit 4)

Impact: Direct driver of AI recommendation

Priority 2: UGC Presence High

  • Monitor and engage on Quora pet food threads
  • Build authentic Reddit presence in r/IndianPets
  • Encourage customer testimonials on these platforms

Impact: Second-largest citation source

Priority 3: Own-Site Content Medium

  • Create HTML comparison tables (not just JSON-LD)
  • Build "Best X for Y" content Google can extract
  • Include competitor products in comparisons

Impact: Increases citation likelihood

Deprioritize

JSON-LD/Schema Implementation: Move from "Critical" to "Low" priority. Still valuable for traditional SEO rich snippets, but does not drive AI Overview visibility. Implement as hygiene, not strategy.

Conclusion

Three things HUFT must internalize to win in the AI-driven discovery landscape

1

JSON-LD is not the answer

Our correlation study found zero relationship between structured data and AI Overview citation. Deprioritize schema work; focus on content and distribution.

2

Third parties control your fate

Google cites comparison sites and UGC platforms, then extracts brand recommendations from their tables. HUFT must appear in these sources to be recommended.

3

Citation ≠ Recommendation

Royal Canin is cited 0 times but recommended in 87% of AI Overviews. The goal is to be in the content Google extracts from, not to be the source URL.

The brands that dominate AI Overviews today will shape consumer consideration sets tomorrow.
HUFT has 90 days to shift from organic search leader to AI discovery leader—or cede that ground to competitors.
