5. The Entity Coherence Problem Page 7

Why AI Systems Distrust Natural Balance

AI systems don't just count brand mentions—they evaluate consistency across sources. When a brand tells a coherent story across multiple channels, AI gains confidence in recommending it. Natural Balance's fragmented presence sends weak signals.

Exhibit 7: How AI Evaluates Brand Authority

The Entity Coherence Principle

When AI systems see the same brand information confirmed across multiple independent sources—website, Amazon, reviews, expert content, user discussions—they treat that brand as more authoritative and trustworthy. Complementary sources create a positive signal. Fragmented or inconsistent presence creates doubt.

Natural Balance's Signal Fragmentation

Source Type Blue Buffalo Natural Balance AI Signal Strength

Expert Content Vet mentions, nutritionist citations Limited expert endorsement Weak

Google Organic SEO 42% visibility 12% visibility Weak

The Single Source of Truth Problem

Natural Balance lacks a Single Source of Truth (SSOT) for its brand online. Without strong owned properties:

What Happens Without SSOT

  • Retailers control product descriptions
  • Third parties define brand narrative
  • Inconsistent messaging across channels
  • AI sees fragmented, low-confidence signals
  • Brand can't correct misinformation

What Competitors Do Right

  • Rich owned website as authoritative source
  • Consistent messaging: web → Amazon → reviews
  • Active content seeding across channels
  • AI sees reinforcing signals everywhere
  • Brand controls the narrative
"AI systems are pattern matchers. When they see the same story told consistently across 10 sources, they believe it. When they see fragments and gaps, they recommend someone else."

The Coherence Gap: Natural Balance vs. Leaders

Blue Buffalo appears in 42% of Google organic results AND 72% of OpenAI responses AND 46% of ChatGPT Web. This consistency across surfaces reinforces AI confidence. Natural Balance's numbers—12%, 25%, 12%—show a brand that hasn't achieved entity coherence.

6. Strategic Recommendations Page 8

The Path Forward

Natural Balance needs a comprehensive digital visibility strategy across three fronts: AI presence, Amazon optimization, and direct-to-consumer capabilities.

1
AI Visibility Campaign: "Own the Limited Ingredient Conversation" Critical Priority

What: Systematically increase Natural Balance's presence in the content ecosystem that AI models learn from—expert reviews, comparison articles, user-generated content, and authoritative pet nutrition resources.

Why: AI systems recommend brands they've "learned about" from training data. Blue Buffalo appears in 72% of AI responses because their brand saturates the web.

How:

  • Pitch Natural Balance to 20+ pet food review sites for inclusion in "best limited ingredient dog food" articles
  • Partner with veterinary nutritionists for authoritative content
  • Launch influencer program targeting pet health content creators
  • Seed discussions on Reddit (r/dogs, r/dogfood) and pet forums

Expected Impact: 15-25 percentage point improvement in AI visibility within 6-12 months

2
Amazon Optimization Program Critical Priority

What: Overhaul Amazon listings with AI-optimized content, enhanced A+ pages, and strategic keyword targeting for Rufus recommendations.

Why: Amazon Rufus influences purchase decisions at the moment of transaction. Current 4% visibility is unacceptable for a brand of Natural Balance's caliber.

How:

  • Rewrite all product bullets with specific, quantifiable claims ("12 real food ingredients", "0 artificial preservatives", "100% grain-free")
  • Optimize A+ content for Rufus extraction—use clear tables, comparison charts, and benefit lists
  • Target "limited ingredient" keywords in backend search terms
  • Invest in Amazon Ads for category visibility

Expected Impact: 2-3x improvement in Rufus recommendations; measurable sales lift

6. Strategic Recommendations (continued) Page 9
3
Launch Direct-to-Consumer E-Commerce High Priority

What: Add e-commerce functionality to naturalbalanceinc.com, enabling direct sales, subscription programs, and first-party data collection.

Why: 6 competitors already sell direct. Without DTC, Natural Balance: (a) loses margin to retailers, (b) has no customer data, (c) can't build subscription revenue, (d) can't control the brand experience.

How:

  • Implement Shopify or similar e-commerce platform
  • Launch with subscription option (auto-ship) for recurring revenue
  • Integrate loyalty program with purchase tracking
  • Capture email for remarketing and product launches

Expected Impact: New revenue channel; customer data asset; reduced retailer dependence

4
Content Marketing: Become the Limited Ingredient Authority High Priority

What: Create definitive content about limited ingredient diets that AI systems will reference when answering related queries.

Why: The brand that "owns" a topic in AI systems wins long-term. Natural Balance should be the default answer for limited ingredient questions.

How:

  • Publish "The Complete Guide to Limited Ingredient Dog Food" as authoritative resource
  • Create ingredient transparency content (what's in each product, why)
  • Partner with veterinary schools for research citations
  • Develop comparison content: "Limited Ingredient vs. Grain-Free: What's the Difference?"
5
Google AI Overview Optimization Medium Priority

What: Structure website content for extraction by Google's AI Overview feature.

Why: 6% visibility in Google AI Overview is critically low. AI Overviews appear above organic results and influence consumer consideration.

How:

  • Add FAQ structured data to product pages
  • Create HTML comparison tables Google can extract
  • Optimize for featured snippet queries ("what is limited ingredient dog food?")

Conclusion Page 11

Three Things to Remember

As you consider this report and discuss with your team, we leave you with three essential takeaways.

1

Natural Balance is invisible to AI.

With 4-25% visibility across AI surfaces, Natural Balance is being outcompeted 3-18x by Blue Buffalo and other leaders. When consumers ask AI "what's the best dog food?", Natural Balance is rarely part of the answer—even for limited ingredient queries where it should dominate.

Appendix A: Methodology Page 11

Research Methodology

Data Collection

Component Details
Query Set 100 US dog food queries covering: brand comparisons, breed-specific, health conditions, life stages, shopping intent
AI Surfaces Studied 8 surfaces: ChatGPT Web, OpenAI API (Chat Completions), OpenAI Responses API, Gemini API, Google AI Overview, Google Organic, Bing Search, Amazon Rufus
Brands Tracked 17 brands including Natural Balance, Blue Buffalo, Purina, Royal Canin, Hill's, Wellness, and competitors
Amazon Content Analysis 6 brands with direct website shopping: Blue Buffalo, Canidae, Nutro, Taste of the Wild, Pedigree, Iams
Website Shopping Audit 17 brand websites checked for e-commerce functionality
Study Date January 2026

Brand Detection Methodology

  • Case-insensitive matching: All brand name variations counted
  • Counting method: Binary presence per query (brand mentioned = 1)
  • Visibility calculation: % of queries where brand appears in response

Amazon Rufus Data Collection

  • Chrome browser with Amazon session, connected via CDP
  • Rufus panel activated for each query
  • Response text captured and analyzed for brand mentions
  • 100 queries executed with human-like delays

Limitations

  • Point-in-time: AI systems evolve; results may shift
  • Query sample: 100 queries may not capture full search demand
  • Rufus variability: Amazon Rufus responses vary by user history
  • US market only: Results may differ in other geographies
