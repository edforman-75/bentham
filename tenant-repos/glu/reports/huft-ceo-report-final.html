What We Found

The Problem

Royal Canin appears in 67% of AI Overviews. Pedigree in 63%. Drools in 57%. HUFT appears in just 11%. As more searches end at AI summaries, HUFT's hard-won organic rankings become less valuable.

The Opportunity

Our research identified the root cause: it's not about technical SEO. It's about third-party presence. Google cites comparison sites and extracts brand recommendations from their content. HUFT must be in those tables.

The Counter-Intuitive Finding

The emerging "Generative Engine Optimization" industry claims that JSON-LD structured data is critical for AI visibility. Our research found no evidence for this.

We audited 78 websites and found that sites cited in AI Overviews have identical JSON-LD rates to non-cited sites (76.9% vs 76.9%). Major platforms like Quora, YouTube, and even RoyalCanin.com have no JSON-LD yet dominate AI results. The path to AI visibility is not through schema markup—it's through being mentioned in the sources Google trusts.

Google's responses are shorter and more conservative than the underlying model.

Strategic implication: HUFT should prioritize ChatGPT visibility (where web layer augmentation helps) while recognizing that Google AI Overview visibility requires being in the curated, conservative citation layer—not just the foundation model's training data.

4. The JSON-LD Myth Page 7

Challenging Industry Assumptions

The emerging "Generative Engine Optimization" (GEO) industry claims that structured data—particularly JSON-LD schema markup—is critical for AI visibility. We tested this claim rigorously.

The Statistical Verdict

Chi-square statistic: 0.000   |   p-value: > 0.10   |   Correlation: None

Sites with JSON-LD structured data are no more likely to be cited in AI Overviews than sites without it. The correlation is statistically indistinguishable from zero.

Counter-Evidence: Success Without Schema

Several of the most-cited sources in AI Overviews have no JSON-LD whatsoever:

User Platforms

  • Quora.com
  • Reddit.com
  • YouTube.com

Social Media

  • Facebook.com
  • Instagram.com

Brand Sites

  • RoyalCanin.com
  • BlueCross.org.uk

The Critical Insight

Royal Canin's website has NO JSON-LD schema markup—yet the brand appears in 87% of AI Overviews. This proves conclusively that schema markup is not the driver of AI visibility. Royal Canin wins because it's mentioned in the third-party content that Google trusts, not because of technical SEO implementation.

Revised Strategic Implication

JSON-LD implementation should be deprioritized from "Critical" to "Low" for AI visibility purposes. It remains valuable for traditional SEO (rich snippets, Google Shopping), but investing heavily in schema as an AI strategy would be misallocated capital.

The Three Drivers

1
Third-Party Comparison Tables

Google extracts brand recommendations from HTML tables on comparison sites. URL fragments in citations show Table_title and Table_content extraction. Being in these tables = being recommended.

Evidence: Royal Canin cited 2x but appears in 87% of AI Overviews—all mentions extracted from third-party tables.

2
User-Generated Content

Quora and Reddit are among the most-cited sources. Google trusts authentic user discussions and extracts brand recommendations from them. Authentic presence on these platforms drives AI visibility.

Evidence: Quora has 0 JSON-LD yet is the #2 most-cited source.

3
Extractable Content Structure

Content structured as HTML tables, numbered lists, and clear headings is more likely to be extracted by AI. This is about HTML semantics, not schema markup.

Evidence: "Best X for Y" articles with table formatting are disproportionately cited.

The Strategic Reframe

HUFT's AI visibility problem is not a technical SEO problem—it's a distribution problem. The solution is not implementing schema; it's ensuring HUFT appears in the third-party content that Google trusts and cites.

6. Strategic Recommendations Page 9

The Path Forward

Based on our research, we recommend a fundamental shift in HUFT's digital strategy: from technical optimization to third-party influence.

1
Secure Third-Party Comparison Table Presence Critical Priority

What: Systematically ensure HUFT products appear in comparison tables on the sites Google cites most frequently: supertails.com, petsworld.in, petindiaonline.com, zigly.com.

Why: This is the primary mechanism by which Google extracts brand recommendations for AI Overviews.

How: Partnership outreach, advertising, content collaboration. Goal: presence in 10+ comparison tables within 90 days.

Expected Impact: 15-25 percentage point increase in AI Overview presence

2
Build Authentic UGC Presence Critical Priority

What: Establish genuine presence on Quora (pet food threads) and Reddit (r/IndianPets). Not astroturfing—real engagement by knowledgeable staff.

Why: Quora is the #2 most-cited source in AI Overviews. Authentic discussions mentioning HUFT will be extracted.

How: Assign 1-2 team members as community experts. Answer questions genuinely. Encourage satisfied customers to share experiences.

Expected Impact: 5-10 percentage point increase in AI visibility; long-term brand authority

3
Create Extractable Comparison Content High Priority

What: Publish comparison content on headsupfortails.com that includes competitor products in honest, table-formatted comparisons.

Why: Google extracts from tables. If HUFT's own content includes well-structured comparisons, it may be cited.

How: "HUFT vs Royal Canin" guides, "Best Dog Food for [Breed]" with comparison tables, pricing matrices.

Expected Impact: Increased likelihood of citation as source; improved content authority
