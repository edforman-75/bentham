Amazon Analysis Page 4

The Amazon Strategy Question

Deckers has deliberately stayed out of Amazon—a strategic choice that made sense while building premium brand positioning. But third-party resellers now sell HOKA and UGG on Amazon with inconsistent descriptions, and this fragmentation hurts Deckers visibility across all AI surfaces, not just Amazon.

The Historical Rationale

  • Protect premium brand positioning
  • Maintain DTC channel control
  • Avoid Amazon's margin pressure
  • Prevent brand dilution during growth phase
  • Focus on owned retail experience

The Current Reality

  • 3P resellers sell HOKA/UGG on Amazon anyway
  • Product descriptions differ from Deckers' official messaging
  • Inconsistent data fragments brand entity signals
  • AI systems trained on Amazon data see conflicting information
  • Competitors (Brooks) control their Amazon narrative

Why This Matters Beyond Amazon

Amazon and Google are foundational data sources for AI systems. When ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Gemini answer product queries, they draw on web data that includes Amazon listings. Inconsistent 3P descriptions on Amazon don't just hurt Rufus—they create entity fragmentation that degrades Deckers' visibility across every AI surface.

The Strategic Question

The "no Amazon" strategy made sense while HOKA and UGG were establishing premium positioning. Strategic Recommendations Page 8

Four Priority Actions

1. Evaluate Formal Amazon Presence

Priority: Critical | Timeline: Q1 Strategic Decision

The "no Amazon" strategy made sense during brand establishment. Now the question is whether continued absence serves Deckers better than controlled presence. Consider:

  • Option A: Formal Entry — Official Brand Stores with A+ Content, authorized seller policy, consistent messaging that aligns with DTC
  • Option B: Enhanced Control — Remain off-Amazon but aggressively police 3P listings, enforce Brand Registry rights, ensure description consistency
  • Option C: Status Quo — Accept 3P fragmentation as cost of premium positioning

Key Insight: Amazon data feeds AI systems everywhere. Inconsistent 3P descriptions don't just hurt Amazon visibility—they degrade entity coherence across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini.

2. Product Data Optimization

Priority: Critical | Timeline: 30-60 Days

AI systems (especially Rufus) rely on structured product data. Ensure:

  • Consistent brand names across all Amazon ASINs
  • Complete product attributes (materials, use cases, features)
  • High-quality images with lifestyle context
  • Category-appropriate keywords in titles and bullets

3. Protect AI Leadership

Priority: High | Timeline: Ongoing

HOKA and UGG's AI visibility is a competitive moat. Protect it by:

  • Monitoring AI surfaces monthly for visibility trends
  • Ensuring website content is AI-crawlable and structured
  • Maintaining strong earned media presence (reviews, expert coverage)
  • Responding to brand comparison queries (HOKA vs Brooks, UGG vs Bearpaw)

4. Teva Competitive Defense

Priority: High | Timeline: Q2 2026

Teva faces strong competition from Keen and Merrell. Strengthen position by:

  • Increasing adventure/outdoor content marketing
  • Targeting summer season queries proactively
  • Building presence on outdoor-focused platforms
Conclusion Page 9

The Path Forward

Deckers has built exceptional AI visibility for HOKA and UGG through strong brand equity, quality products, and controlled web presence. The Amazon absence was a deliberate strategy—but with 3P sellers fragmenting brand data on a platform that feeds all AI systems, it's time to ask whether that strategy still serves Deckers.

The Core Insight

This study is about web presence, not just AI surfaces. Amazon and Google are foundational data sources—when 3P resellers describe HOKA differently than Deckers does, that inconsistency propagates to ChatGPT, Perplexity, and every other AI system trained on web data. The question isn't whether to "fix" Amazon—it's whether to control the narrative there.

The Foundational Data Source Problem

Amazon and Google aren't just discovery surfaces—they're foundational data sources that feed every AI system. When users ask ChatGPT or Perplexity about running shoes, those systems draw on training data that includes Amazon product descriptions. Inconsistent 3P listings don't just hurt Amazon visibility—they inject conflicting signals into the entire AI ecosystem.

The AI visibility leadership HOKA and UGG have built came from controlling their web narrative. The question now: Can that leadership be sustained when Amazon—the largest product database on the web—tells a fragmented story?
