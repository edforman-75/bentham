**Prepared by:** Bentham Research

## Executive Summary

HUFT's web presence is well-optimized for how metro Indian consumers have historically discovered products—through Google Search. However, the discovery landscape is shifting, and HUFT faces an emerging vulnerability in AI-generated summaries that increasingly intercept the traditional search journey.

**The core finding:** HUFT wins on the surfaces that matter most today, but is underrepresented on the surfaces that will matter most tomorrow.

---

## How Metro Indian Consumers Discover Pet Products

### The Search Landscape

Google dominates product discovery in India with approximately **95% search market share**. For metro consumers researching pet products, the typical journey is:

1. **Google Search** — The starting point for 85%+ of product research
2. **E-commerce platforms** — Amazon.in, Flipkart for price comparison and purchase
3. **Social media** — Instagram, YouTube for reviews and recommendations
4. **AI chatbots** — ChatGPT, Gemini for conversational queries (growing but still <5% of discovery)

### Surface Usage Estimates for Pet Product Discovery (Metro India)

| Surface | Estimated Share of Discovery | HUFT Visibility | Gap |
|---------|------------------------------|-----------------|-----|
| Google Search (organic results) | 60% | **75-85%** | Strong |
| Google AI Overviews | 15% (and growing) | **11%** | Critical weakness |
| E-commerce search (Amazon, etc.) | 15% | Not measured | Unknown |
| ChatGPT / AI chatbots | 5% | **70-75%** | Strong |

## What Our Data Reveals

### HUFT's Current Position: Strong Where It Counts

For a metro Indian consumer searching Google from Mumbai or Delhi, HUFT appears prominently:

- **75-85% of organic searches** show HUFT in the top 10 results
- **50% of searches** show HUFT in the top 3 organic results
- headsupfortails.com ranks competitively against Supertails, Zigly, and international brands

This is a defensible position built on years of SEO investment, content marketing, and brand authority.

### The AI Overview Problem

Google is rapidly expanding AI Overviews—the AI-generated summary boxes that appear above organic results. Our data shows:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Queries showing AI Overviews | 84% of pet product searches |
| HUFT appearance in AI Overviews | **11%** |
| Royal Canin appearance in AI Overviews | 67% |
| Pedigree appearance in AI Overviews | 63% |
| Drools appearance in AI Overviews | 57% |

**Why this matters:** Research indicates that 60% of Google searches now end without a click—users get their answer from the AI Overview and never scroll to organic results. If this trend continues:

- HUFT's strong organic rankings become less valuable
- Competitors appearing in AI Overviews capture the "zero-click" discovery moment
- Users form brand impressions from AI summaries, not from visiting headsupfortails.com

### Where Competitors Beat HUFT in AI

This table shows the critical gap: competitors dominate the AI surfaces that increasingly determine brand visibility.

| Surface | HUFT | Royal Canin | Pedigree | Drools | Winner |
|---------|------|-------------|----------|--------|--------|
| **Google AI Overview** | **11%** | **67%** | **63%** | **57%** | **Competitors** |
| Google Organic (India) | 75-85% | 45% | 40% | 50% | HUFT |
| ChatGPT Web (India) | 70-75% | 50% | 55% | 45% | HUFT |
| Gemini API (no suffix) | 15% | 60% | 55% | 50% | **Competitors** |
| Gemini API ("in India") | 85% | 40% | 35% | 45% | HUFT |

**Key insight: HUFT loses on the two surfaces that matter most for passive discovery:**

1. **Google AI Overview (11% vs 60%+)**: This is the most critical gap. 84% of pet product searches show AI Overviews, and 60% of users don't click through to organic results. Competitors are 5-6x more visible than HUFT in this surface.

2. **Gemini API without geographic context (15% vs 50-60%)**: When queries don't include "in India," Gemini returns international brands. This matters for third-party apps, voice assistants, and AI tools that use Gemini without passing location context.

**Where HUFT wins:**
- Google Organic from India (75-85%) - traditional SEO strength
- ChatGPT Web from India (70-75%) - good consumer AI presence
- Any surface with explicit "in India" context (85-90%)

**The strategic problem:** HUFT only wins when users explicitly signal India context or click through to organic results. As AI summaries become the primary discovery interface, HUFT's advantages erode.

### The Query Localization Effect

A striking pattern emerged across all surfaces: explicit India context dramatically improves HUFT visibility.

| Query Type | Google (India IP) | ChatGPT (India IP) | Gemini API |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|------------|
| Original query | 70-75% | 70% | 15% |
| With "in India" suffix | **85-90%** | 75% | **85%** |

This suggests HUFT's brand identity is strongly anchored to India-specific context in AI training data. Generic queries (without geographic context) return international brands even when the user is clearly in India.

**Implication:** A user searching "best dog food brands" sees Pedigree, Royal Canin, and Drools. A user searching "best dog food brands in India" sees HUFT. Most users search generically.

## Platform-by-Platform Analysis

### Google Search: The Primary Battleground

**Current state:** HUFT has strong organic presence but weak AI Overview presence.

**Risk level:** HIGH — As AI Overviews expand, organic rankings lose influence.

**What's happening:** Google's AI appears to favor:
- International brands with extensive web presence (Royal Canin, Pedigree)
- Brands with strong structured data and schema markup
- Brands frequently mentioned in authoritative content (vet recommendations, review sites)

HUFT's organic strength comes from headsupfortails.com's direct rankings, but AI Overviews synthesize information from multiple sources where HUFT may be less prominent.

### ChatGPT: Strong but Niche

**Current state:** 70-75% HUFT visibility from India IP.

**Risk level:** LOW (for now) — ChatGPT usage for product discovery in India is still small.

**What's happening:** ChatGPT's training data and/or real-time web search includes strong HUFT signals when accessed from India. The consumer web interface shows significantly better HUFT representation than the underlying APIs.

**Future consideration:** As AI assistants become embedded in more applications (WhatsApp, e-commerce, customer service), the API visibility gap could become relevant.

### Gemini: Highly Context-Dependent

**Current state:** 15% visibility without "in India" suffix, 85% with it.

**Risk level:** MEDIUM — Gemini powers Google's AI features and third-party integrations.

**What's happening:** Gemini API does not infer geographic context from IP location the way Google Search does. Without explicit India context in the query, Gemini returns globally-oriented recommendations dominated by international brands.

## Strategic Implications

### The Attention Shift

Metro Indian consumers are experiencing a fundamental shift in how they receive information:

| Era | Discovery Pattern | HUFT Position |
|-----|-------------------|---------------|
| 2015-2023 | Search → Click → Browse → Decide | Strong (organic SEO) |
| 2024-2026 | Search → AI Summary → Decide (no click) | Weak (AI Overview gap) |
| 2026+ | AI Assistant → Recommendation → Purchase | Mixed (ChatGPT strong, APIs weak) |

HUFT's current strength is optimized for the first pattern. The second pattern is already dominant and the third is emerging.

### The Zero-Click Challenge

When a Mumbai consumer searches "best dog food for Labrador," they increasingly see:

1. **AI Overview** (Royal Canin, Pedigree, Drools mentioned) — User reads this
2. **Sponsored results** (competitors may bid on these terms)
3. **Organic results** (HUFT appears here) — User may never scroll this far

The investment in organic SEO that made HUFT visible is being bypassed by AI summaries where HUFT is underrepresented.

### The Trust Signal Gap

AI systems appear to weight certain signals heavily:
- Veterinary endorsements and recommendations
- Structured product data (schema.org markup)
- Presence on authoritative third-party sites
- Review aggregation and ratings

HUFT may be underrepresented in these signal categories relative to competitors like Royal Canin (strong vet channel) and Pedigree (massive brand awareness).

### Critical Insight: How Google AI Overviews Actually Work

Our data reveals exactly how Google constructs AI Overviews—and it's not what most SEO advice suggests.

#### Finding 1: Brand websites are rarely cited; third-party aggregators dominate

| Source Domain | Times Cited in AI Overview | Brand Mentioned in AI Overview |
|---------------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **amazon.in** | 93 | (multiple brands) |
| **supertails.com** | 58 | HUFT, Royal Canin, Pedigree, etc. |
| **headsupfortails.com** | 38 | HUFT |
| **zigly.com** | 28 | Various |
| **mrnmrspet.com** | 9 | Various (comparison tables) |
| **royalcanin.com** | **0** | Royal Canin (67% of AI Overviews) |
| **pedigree.com** | **3** | Pedigree (63% of AI Overviews) |

**Key insight:** Royal Canin and Pedigree are cited in 60%+ of AI Overviews as **brand recommendations**, but their own websites are almost **never used as sources**.

Google synthesizes brand recommendations from:
- Third-party comparison articles
- Retailer product listings
- Review aggregators
- "Best of" listicles

**Implication for HUFT:** Improving headsupfortails.com's structured data helps with **being cited as a source**, but to **be recommended as a brand**, HUFT needs to appear prominently in third-party comparison content.

#### Finding 2: Google extracts HTML tables for structured data

The AI Overview source URLs contain text fragments showing exactly what Google extracts:

```
supertails.com#:~:text=Table_title:%20Dog%20Dry%20Food%20Price%20List%20Table_content:...Price%20(INR):%20₹781
mrnmrspet.com#:~:text=Table_title:%20Quick%20Comparison%20Table%20Table_content:...Key%20Highlight:%20Grain-free
amazon.in#:~:text=Table_title:%20Product%20specifications%20Table_content:...Brand...Ingredients
```

**Google is extracting:**
- HTML table content (not just JSON-LD schema)
- Table titles for context
- Specific cell values (prices, features, ingredients)
- Comparison data across brands

**Implication for HUFT:**
1. Create comparison tables on headsupfortails.com that include HUFT + competitors
2. Ensure HUFT is included in third-party comparison tables
3. Use semantic HTML tables with clear headers, not just styled divs

#### Finding 3: The "source citation" vs "brand mention" gap

| Brand | Cited as Source | Mentioned as Recommendation | Gap |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------------|-----|
| HUFT (headsupfortails.com) | 38 times | 11% of AI Overviews | Source > Mention |
| Royal Canin | 0 times | 67% of AI Overviews | Mention > Source |
| Supertails | 58 times | ~20% of AI Overviews | Source > Mention |

**What this means:**
- Being a **source** (website cited) ≠ Being a **recommendation** (brand mentioned)
- Royal Canin succeeds without its own site being cited because other sources recommend it
- HUFT is cited as a retailer/source but not recommended as a brand within those sources

## Conclusion

HUFT has built a strong web presence for the search paradigm that dominated the past decade. Metro Indian consumers searching Google will frequently encounter HUFT in organic results.

However, the discovery landscape is shifting beneath this foundation:

1. **AI Overviews are intercepting search traffic** — 60% of searches end without a click, and HUFT appears in only 11% of these AI summaries versus 60%+ for major competitors.

2. **Geographic context is not automatic** — AI systems don't consistently recognize India context from IP alone, disadvantaging HUFT when users search generically.

3. **Category perception is limited** — HUFT is visible for treats and accessories but invisible for core dog food recommendations, suggesting a brand positioning gap.

The strategic imperative is clear: HUFT must optimize for AI surfaces with the same intensity it has applied to organic SEO. The brands that appear in AI Overviews today will shape consumer consideration sets tomorrow—and HUFT is currently ceding that ground to international and budget competitors.

The good news: this is an optimization problem, not a brand awareness problem. HUFT has the underlying presence; the task is ensuring that presence translates into AI visibility.
