Strategic Consideration: The Amazon Discovery Gap

Choosing not to sell on Amazon may be a sound channel management decision to protect brand positioning and margins. However, it creates a significant discovery gap:

  • 50-61% of consumers start product research on Amazon - if TASC isn't there, these consumers may never discover the brand
  • Even ROPO shoppers who prefer to buy in-store (49% of men's apparel buyers) often begin their research on Amazon
  • Competitors Nike, Under Armour, and Rhone have Amazon Brand Stores - they capture this research traffic even from consumers who may buy elsewhere
  • Premium brands Lululemon and Vuori also avoid Amazon - but they have stronger brand awareness and retail distribution to offset this

Activewear Category ROPO Behavior

Activewear falls into the category where ROPO behavior is strongest. Consumers want to:

  • Feel the fabric - bamboo's softness is a key differentiator that's hard to convey online
  • Try on for fit - athletic apparel sizing varies significantly by brand
  • See colors in person - screen colors don't always match reality
  • Get immediate gratification - take the product home same day

This suggests TASC's target customer may be particularly likely to research online but prefer to purchase in-store or from trusted DTC sites.

Implication for TASC

TASC's strong Google and ChatGPT visibility (80% and 79%) is critical because these are primary research channels. However, missing from Amazon means missing consumers who start their journey there. Consider whether an Amazon presence - even limited - could serve as a discovery funnel that drives traffic to tascperformance.com or retail partners.
